About the Project

Conflict

  • Adults struggle to save money due to a lack of financial literacy.
  • Consequently, they are unable to save money in a structured way.
  • This highlights the importance of starting financial education at a young age.

The Big Idea

  • The idea is to educate teenagers (13-18) through a budgeting app with parental control.
  • It will focus on goal setting and the 50/30/20 budgeting rule, to foster responsible financial habits from a young age.

FINAL SCREENS

How might we teach finance?

Interactive Learning

  • Complete quizzes to unlock surprises, incorporating gamified elements for an engaging learning journey.
  • With this innovative approach, I'm actively addressing the challenge of money management education for teenagers.
  • By placing video tutorials on the home screen, teenagers are motivated to watch and take quizzes to unlock surprises.

Add money into your goals

Check Your Balance:

  • Easily view your goal and card balance when adding money.

No More Requests

  • Requesting money from parents is deactivated when you have funds in your card balance.”

Surprise Motivation

  • Reach your goal and receive a non-monetary surprise from your parents to boost your savings motivation.

How might we encourage teenagers to save money?

Think Twice Before Delete

  • Since this is the stage for a 13-year-old teenager, they are asked to request deletion from their parent.
  • Transfers are meant to motivate them to keep saving instead of spending from their balance.

Words of a 16-Year-Old

  • "I like this feature because every time I try to save money to buy something, I end up spending it when I don't have enough allowance. When I spend too much, my parents tell me to use my savings.”

SOLUTION & IMPACT

Social impact

  • Financial literacy education will be enhanced at a young age.
  • The interactive gamification between parent and child will increase learning.

Future scope

  • Educational mechanics can be improved.
  • Detailed parent view will be created.
  • KPI’s should be tested such as task success rate, time on task and system usability scale.
  • Another version for kids 6-12 could created with more playful interface.

Learning curve

  • Constant feedback and iteration through usability testing improve the design.
  • Designing according to user’s needs and feedback is crucial.
